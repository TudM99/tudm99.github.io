Blender Add-on Revshell

Using a Blender add-on to gain a reverse shell.

Hey there! Recently I started delving into 3D again, figured I'd take a break from cyber for the weekend. Last night though, I wanted to finish rendering an animation I've been working on, but I was really tired, so I looked around for an addon to shutdown my PC after completing the render.

Found the addon, saw the .py file, and immediately my mind went "put a shell in it". And so I did. I woke up this morning, uninstalled the add-on, modified it with a revshell, and installed it back in.

As you can see in the video below, defender didn't make a fuss, and the shell worked perfectly on the receiving end. The only issue is blender will stop responding while the shell is active. But blender being blender, most users with an important project open will wait for it to respond, giving you enough time to use the netcat shell to upload a more robust, unrelated (to blender) shell.

If we take into consideration the fact that most initial attacks nowadays are automated, with a separate shell being instantly uploaded and calling back to the C2 once the initial compromise is successful, I don't think blender's responsiveness, or lack thereof, is gonna be an issue for a potential attacker.

If you're working with community add-ons, please be careful and inspect them a bit before installing / using them. Yes, my shell may disturb blender's functionality, but it's rudimentary, easy to spot, and not implemented with stealth in mind.

Take care!
